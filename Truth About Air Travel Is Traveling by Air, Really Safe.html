Is Traveling by Air, Really Safe?

 

©boyslife.org

  

 

By Michael Gonzalez
 

There were many airplanes traveling before the start of the pandemic. The risk of being in an airplane accident is very low. If you look on social media then you will see a large amount of reports that state air travel is dangerous. If we can look any data and it might gives more information about safety.
 
 
©freeimages.com
                         

As safety measures goes forward commercial air travel has become safer over time. There have fewer report of accidents that involve airplanes. Though a low accident rate is being taken from the metrics of (Airliner Accidents) and (Airliner Fatalities).  Over the years the numbers of accidents and fatalities have gone up and down.  Looking at the over period of time, you can see that the rate of fatalities are generally lower.[1] This is a good sign to see when you are wondering about air travel accidents and fatalities.


In the years of 1985 to 1999, it was about 7 thousand deaths and it was a dangerous time to travel by air. While for the years of 2000 to 2014 had about 3.5 thousand deaths was a safer time to travel by air.[3]
 
 
 
Are there other safer ways to Travel ?

 

Speaking about accidents in the air, when compared with accidents in other means of traveling, it seems to be a smaller problem. Now we will compare the accident rates of air travel with car accident rates from the NHTSA website. Hopefully, we can see if air travel is safer than other ways of traveling.
 
 
©porsche-mania.com      
 

Generally speaking, driving on the highway is one of the most dangerous way to travel. Drivers have a higher chance of dying in a motor vehicle crash, when comparing to other ways of traveling. On the NHTSA website, it states that on average of  33,654 fatalities happen per year.[2] Comparing this to the Aviation Safety Network website, there has not been any fatalities close enough to the NHTSA statistic. So, why does everyone think that air travel is more dangerous?



In the years of 1985 to 1999, it was about 0.7 million deaths and it was a dangerous time to travel by car. While for the years of 2000 to 2014 had about 0.6 million deaths which is still dangerous to travel by car.[4]



 Why are we attracted to airplane accidents?



Airplane accidents grab our attention with fear and stick in our minds. It gives us the false impression that these events are common occurrences and even thought it rarely happens. Also the fact that one story of an airplane accident can share many times over and being constantly exposed to these stories will affect your judgment about air travel.



©bloggedtopics.com



As part of this fascination, one airplane crash can cost hundreds of lives. Safety is completely under the pilot's control with passengers not having any control.  When driving in a car you or a close person have control. If you are not driving, you at least have a sense of hope that you will be able to keep control inside the car. Driving is an action that is done on a daily basis and many people think it is a safer option.



Conclusion



If you are thinking about safety in air travel? Then  you can thank the people who work in flight status monitoring, air traffic control and airport safety. This is one of the reasons for low accident rates in air travel. It brings a safer way to travel from checking in at the gate to boarding the airplane. Air travel is still the safest way to travel.
